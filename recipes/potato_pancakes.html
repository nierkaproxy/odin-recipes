Potato pancakes

Description

Lithuanian Potato Pancakes, known as "Bulviniai Blynai," are a delightful Eastern European dish. Grated potatoes are mixed with spices and a touch of olive oil creating a batter that's pan-fried to crispy golden perfection. These pancakes boast a satisfying blend of textures, with a crispy exterior and a tender, flavorful interior. Serve them with a dollop of sour cream or applesauce for a traditional and comforting Lithuanian culinary experience.

Ingredients (for around 25 pancakes or 5-6 portions)

For pancakes:

  • 2 kg potatoes
  • 100 ml olive oil (including for frying)
  • 1,5 tablespoons of salt
  • 0,5 teaspoon of black pepper

For the sauce:

  • 400 g of sour cream
  • 150 g of curd
  • A handful of fresh dill
  • A touch of salt and pepper

Recipe

  1. Peel all the potatoes, and then in a large bowl grate them with an electric potato grater machine, or if you dont have that, use regular grater.
  2. Put 3 tablespoons of olive oil, 1,5 tablespoons of salt and 0,5 teaspoon of black pepper and mix it well
  3. Heat up a large frying pan with some olive oil in it, so that the oil covers all of the pan surface area. And then after the pan is heated up, put 2-3 tablespoons of the batter for each pancake (try to make at least 4 in one pan). Cook until its golden brown, then flip it and do the same for the other side.
  4. While the pancakes are cooking, make the sauce. Finely chop your fresh dill, and in a bowl mix sour cream, curd, chopped dill and salt and pepper.
